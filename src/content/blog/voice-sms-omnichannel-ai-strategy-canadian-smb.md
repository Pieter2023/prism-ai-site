## What Is Omnichannel AI, Really?

"Omnichannel" gets thrown around by software vendors. Strip the buzzword, and it's simple: your prospect can start in any channel (phone, SMS, email) and the conversation continues seamlessly across all of them—powered by AI that remembers context from the first touchpoint to the last.

**Here's how it works in practice:**

| Touchpoint | Traditional Approach | Omnichannel AI Approach |
|------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Initial call | Answered by human or voicemail | AI voice agent answers, qualifies, and sends instant SMS summary |
| Follow-up | Manual email (if remembered) | Automated SMS with booking link sent 5 minutes post-call |
| Appointment reminder | Phone call day before | SMS reminder 24 hours + 1 hour before, with reschedule option |
| No-show recovery | Human has to chase | AI sends SMS offering next available slot automatically |

The difference isn't just convenience. It's continuity. The AI knows what was discussed, what's been promised, and what needs to happen next—regardless of channel.

## Canadian Compliance: What You Need to Know

SMS marketing in Canada operates under CASL (Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation). Here's the practical summary:

**For appointment reminders and service messages:**
- **Implied consent applies** if someone provided their number during a booking or inquiry
- Must include your business name and contact info
- Must provide clear opt-out mechanism ("Reply STOP")

**For marketing/promotional SMS:**
- **Express consent required**—documented opt-in
- Cannot send without prior explicit permission
- Same identification and opt-out requirements

The good news: most SMB use cases (appointment reminders, booking confirmations, follow-ups) fall under implied consent because they're directly related to an existing business relationship. Just keep records and respect STOP requests immediately.

## FAQ: Omnichannel AI for Canadian SMBs

**Do I need to replace my phone system?**
No. AI voice agents can integrate with your existing number and forward calls normally. SMS requires a business texting platform, but these integrate with most CRMs.

**Will customers find SMS follow-ups annoying?**
Not if they're relevant and timely. An immediate booking confirmation? Expected. A promotional text at 10 p.m.? Violation. The key is value and timing.

**What about French-speaking customers?**
In Quebec and bilingual markets, you can configure AI voice agents and SMS flows in French. The same infrastructure supports both languages.

**How much does this cost compared to hiring?**
An AI voice agent + SMS automation stack runs $500–$1,500/month depending on volume. A part-time receptionist costs $2,500–$3,500/month plus benefits. The AI doesn't call in sick, work holidays, or forget to follow up.

**Can I start with just SMS and add voice later?**
Absolutely. Some businesses prefer to start with appointment reminders and follow-up SMS, then add AI voice once they're comfortable with automation.

**What happens when the AI can't handle something?**
Smart escalation. The AI recognises requests outside its scope and either transfers to a human or takes a detailed message with full context. Nothing gets lost.

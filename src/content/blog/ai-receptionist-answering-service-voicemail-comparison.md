A Kelowna HVAC contractor called me last Tuesday. He'd spent the weekend Googling "answering service" after his wife told him they'd missed eleven calls the week before — two of them furnace emergencies billed at $800 a pop.

He had three tabs open: an AI receptionist vendor, Ruby Receptionists, and a blog post titled "How to Set Up Professional Voicemail." He wanted to know which one would actually stop the bleeding.

Here's what I told him.

## The three options you're actually choosing between

When you say "I need to stop missing calls," you're really picking between three things, and the difference between them is larger than most trades businesses realise.

**Option 1: Voicemail** — the free thing your phone company gave you in 2003.

**Option 2: Live answering service** — real humans in a call centre answering on your behalf, billed per minute or per call.

**Option 3: AI receptionist** — a voice agent that answers in milliseconds, books into your calendar, and texts you a summary.

Let's be blunt about what each one actually does.

## Voicemail: free, and worth every penny


Voicemail is free, and 80% of your callers hang up without leaving a message
. 
Research from BIA/Kelsey shows 67% of callers who reach voicemail will not leave a message
 — they just hang up and call the next contractor on Google.


Even when people do leave voicemails, 67% admit to ignoring them entirely, and if the number is unfamiliar, only 18% of people bother listening
.

Think about that for a second. You spend $1,200 on Google Ads to get your phone ringing. The call comes in at 6:15 PM while you're finishing a job. It goes to voicemail. Four out of five callers hang up without a word. The one who does leave a message? You call them back at 8 AM the next morning, and they've already booked with someone who picked up.


Here's the number that should matter: 85% of callers won't try again if you don't answer the first time. They'll call your competitor instead
.

Voicemail isn't a safety net. It's a sieve.

### When voicemail makes sense

Never, if you're trying to capture new business. The only time voicemail works is when the caller already knows you, trusts you, and is willing to wait. Existing clients calling about a scheduled follow-up, sure. New leads calling about a leaky water heater at 9 PM? Gone.

## Live answering services: humans on the line, dollars on the clock

A live answering service puts real people between your phone number and voicemail. When someone calls, a trained agent picks up, follows your script, captures the lead, and texts or emails you the details.

The big names in Canada and the US — Ruby, Smith.ai, AnswerConnect, Responsive — all work roughly the same way. 
Most plans fall between $100 and $1,000+ per month, with smaller plans covering light call volume and basic message taking, while higher tiers support heavier traffic and 24/7 coverage, with pricing tied to usage — minutes, calls, or both
.


AnswerConnect's entry plan costs $350 per month for 200 minutes with a $49.99 setup fee and $2.50 per minute overage, while their growth plan runs $395 per month for 300 minutes with $1.85 per minute overage
. 
Responsive Answering Service starts at $29 per month for flat-rate plans with a one-time setup fee of $75
.

Here's what that looks like in practice. Say you're an electrician getting fifty calls a month. Average call length is three minutes. That's 150 minutes. On a per-minute plan at $1.50 per minute plus a $200 base fee, you're paying $425 a month. Busy month with seventy calls? You're over $500.

### What you get

- Real humans who sound professional and follow your script.
- 24/7 coverage if you pay for it.
- Message capture, appointment booking (if integrated with your calendar), call transfer to your mobile for hot leads.
- Established providers with decades of call-centre experience.

### What you don't get

- Calendar writes. Most services can *check* your calendar if you integrate it, but they won't book the slot without you confirming first. That's a back-and-forth delay.
- Instant answers. Even the best agents take thirty to sixty seconds to pick up during peak times. 
AI answering services resolve 90–95% of calls without escalation and pick up in under 5 seconds, while traditional live services answer in 30–90 seconds
.
- Predictable cost on high-volume weeks. Your April might cost $300; your June might cost $750.

### When a live answering service makes sense

If your calls are complex, require judgment, or involve sensitive information that you don't trust an AI to handle yet, a live service is the move. Law firms, medical clinics, and businesses where the caller's first question is "Can I speak to a real person?" still lean on human agents.

For trades — plumbers, electricians, roofers, HVAC — the value is less clear. Most of your calls are "What do you charge for X?" or "Can you come out Thursday?" An AI handles that just fine.

## AI receptionists: the 2026 answer for trades

An AI receptionist is a voice agent that picks up your business line, holds a real conversation, captures the lead, books into your calendar, and sends you a text summary — all in under two minutes, 24/7, for a flat monthly fee.

You get a dedicated phone number. When someone calls, the AI answers in your brand voice ("Thanks for calling Valley Electric, this is Emma. How can I help you today?"), qualifies the lead, pulls availability from your calendar, books the slot, and confirms via SMS to both you and the caller.

No hold music. No "your call is important to us." No voicemail.


AI answering services in Canada start at around $59 per month for unlimited calling
, though that's for basic packages. 
NextPhone offers flat-rate AI answering at $199 per month with unlimited calls, 20+ languages included, and no per-minute fees or capacity limits
. At Prism AI, a custom-trained AI receptionist with full calendar integration and CRM write-back costs **$4,500 to build and $350 per month to run** (plus third-party telephony costs at our cost, no markup).

### What you get

- Millisecond answer times. No queue, no hold.
- Unlimited simultaneous calls. If ten people call at once, ten agents pick up.
- Calendar integration that actually writes the appointment, no confirmation loop.
- Branded voice and personality trained on your services, your pricing, your FAQ.
- Call transcripts and lead summaries texted to you after every interaction.
- Cost that doesn't scale with volume. Fifty calls cost the same as two hundred.

### What you don't get (yet)

- Perfect empathy. An AI can apologise and sound polite, but it won't pick up on the subtext of a stressed-out homeowner the way a seasoned human agent would.
- Judgment calls on edge cases. If a caller asks something wildly outside your script, the AI will route to you. A human might improvise.
- Universal trust. Some callers — usually older, sometimes burned by bad IVR in the past — will ask "Am I speaking to a real person?" and hang up when you say no.

### When an AI receptionist makes sense

If you're a trades business, a local service shop, a realtor, or a clinic where 80% of inbound calls follow a predictable pattern (pricing, availability, booking), an AI receptionist is the highest-ROI move you can make in 2026.

You're not paying per minute. You're not capped at one call at a time. You're not leaving after-hours calls to voicemail. And you're not spending $6,000 to $12,000 a year on a live service when a $4,500 build and $350 a month gets you better speed, better availability, and better data.
